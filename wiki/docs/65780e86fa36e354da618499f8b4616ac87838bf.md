## Overview
Vector quantization aims to compress high-dimensional Euclidean vectors into low-bitwidth integers while minimizing structural distortion, a core operation for tasks such as KV cache compression in LLMs and nearest neighbor search. Existing methods either lack online applicability or exhibit suboptimal distortion bounds. TurboQuant solves this by presenting a fast, data-oblivious, online vector quantization algorithm that achieves near-optimal distortion rates. It mitigates both Mean-Squared Error (MSE) and inner product distortion, operating efficiently without the heavy preprocessing steps characterizing traditional product quantization techniques.

## Summary
TurboQuant operates on worst-case vectors (normalized to the unit sphere) and fundamentally reshapes their distribution by applying a random rotation matrix $\mathbf{\Pi} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}$. This random projection induces a Beta distribution (which approaches a Gaussian in high dimensions) on every coordinate of $\mathbf{\Pi}\mathbf{x}$, rendering distinct coordinates virtually independent. This allows TurboQuant to apply an optimal 1D continuous Lloyd-Max scalar quantizer independently per coordinate.

For a bit-width $b$, the MSE-optimal quantizer $Q_{mse}$ guarantees a bounded expected distortion tightly matching the theoretical Shannon Lower Bound. Specifically, the distortion is bound by:
$$ D_{mse}(Q_{mse}) := \mathbb{E} \left[ \|\mathbf{x} - Q_{mse}^{-1}(Q_{mse}(\mathbf{x}))\|_2^2 \right] \le \frac{\sqrt{3}\pi}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{4^b} $$

However, MSE-optimal quantizers are biased for inner product estimations. To correct this, the paper introduces a two-stage approach for inner product quantization ($Q_{prod}$):
1. Quantize the rotated vector using an MSE-optimized scalar quantizer at bit-width $b-1$.
2. Apply a 1-bit Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss (QJL) transform on the remaining residual vector $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{x} - \tilde{\mathbf{x}}_{mse}$.

The reconstructed vector yields an unbiased inner product estimator:
$$ \mathbb{E}\left[\langle \mathbf{y}, \tilde{\mathbf{x}}_{mse} + \mathbf{r}_{qjl} \rangle\right] = \langle \mathbf{y}, \mathbf{x} \rangle $$
This method dramatically improves performance in LLM long-context tasks, allowing KV cache compression up to $4.5\times$ (approx. 2.5-3.5 bits per float) without degradation, while massively speeding up inner product approximations for database indexing.

## Glossary
| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Vector Quantization** | The process of mapping a continuous or high-precision multi-dimensional space into a finite set of discrete values or low-precision integers to save memory. |
| **Mean-Squared Error (MSE) Distortion** | A metric capturing the expected squared Euclidean distance between original and quantized vectors. |
| **Shannon Lower Bound (SLB)** | A fundamental information-theoretic lower bound on the optimal achievable distortion rate for any lossy compression scheme. |
| **Data-Oblivious** | An algorithm that functions universally without relying on specific statistics or characteristics of the underlying dataset, making it ideal for online settings. |
| **Product Quantization (PQ)** | A classic nearest-neighbor approach dividing vectors into sub-vectors and quantizing them separately, usually heavily dependent on offline training (k-means). |
