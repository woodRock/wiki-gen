## Overview
LeWorldModel (LeWM) introduces a stable end-to-end method for learning latent world models from raw pixels using a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA). Traditional JEPA approaches for world modeling suffer from instability and representation collapse, often requiring complex multi-term losses or frozen pre-trained encoders. LeWM overcomes this by employing a simple two-term objective: a next-embedding prediction loss and a Sketched-Isotropic-Gaussian Regularizer (SIGReg) that forces the latent embeddings to match a Gaussian distribution. This dramatically simplifies hyperparameter tuning and enables fast, efficient latent planning for complex continuous control tasks.

## Summary
The LeWM framework relies on two primary components: an encoder and a predictor. The encoder maps a raw pixel observation $o_t$ into a compact latent representation $z_t$. The predictor models the environment dynamics autoregressively by predicting the next frame's embedding $\hat{z}_{t+1}$ conditioned on the current state $z_t$ and an action $a_t$:

$$z_t = \text{enc}_{\theta}(o_t)$$

$$\hat{z}_{t+1} = \text{pred}_{\phi}(z_t, a_t)$$

The architecture is trained end-to-end using a remarkably streamlined objective function:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{LeWM}} \triangleq \mathcal{L}_{\text{pred}} + \lambda \text{SIGReg}(\mathbf{Z})$$

Here, $\mathcal{L}_{\text{pred}} \triangleq \|\hat{z}_{t+1} - z_{t+1}\|_2^2$ is the mean-squared error for the dynamics prediction. To prevent the trivial representation collapse typical of JEPA architectures, LeWM introduces **SIGReg**. Because measuring normality in high dimensions is challenging, SIGReg projects the batch of embeddings $\mathbf{Z}$ onto $M$ random 1D directions $u^{(m)} \in \mathbb{S}^{D-1}$ and applies a univariate normality test, the Epps-Pulley test $T(h^{(m)})$:

$$\text{SIGReg}(\mathbf{Z}) \triangleq \frac{1}{M} \sum_{m=1}^M T(h^{(m)})$$

This optimization encourages the full joint distribution to be isotropic Gaussian, naturally preventing collapse. Once trained, LeWM acts as a simulator, allowing agents to perform Model Predictive Control (MPC) using the Cross-Entropy Method (CEM) to optimize sequences of actions entirely within the latent space.

## Glossary
| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **World Model** | A predictive model of an environment's dynamics, allowing an agent to simulate future states in a latent imagination space without executing them in reality. |
| **Representation Collapse** | A trivial solution where an encoder maps all distinct inputs to the exact same point or subspace, effectively rendering the representations useless for prediction or discrimination. |
| **Sketched-Isotropic-Gaussian Regularizer (SIGReg)** | A regularization technique that projects high-dimensional embeddings onto random 1D lines and enforces normality, effectively steering the multi-dimensional distribution towards an isotropic Gaussian. |
| **Latent Planning** | The process of optimizing action sequences by rolling out future trajectories in the low-dimensional space of a world model rather than pixel space. |
