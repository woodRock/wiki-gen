BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) is a language representation model that introduced deep bidirectional pre-training for language understanding. Unlike previous models that processed text in a unidirectional (left-to-right or right-to-left) fashion, BERT is designed to pre-train deep bidirectional representations from unlabeled text by jointly conditioning on both left and right context across all layers. Consequently, the pre-trained BERT model can be fine-tuned with a single additional output layer to achieve state-of-the-art results for a wide range of natural language processing tasks, including question answering and language inference.

Bidirectional Pre-training

The primary technical innovation of BERT is the application of bidirectional training within the Transformer—a widely used attention-based architecture—to language modeling. Previous attempts at bidirectional modeling were limited, as they typically relied on the concatenation of two unidirectional models or constrained the architecture to prevent tokens from 'seeing themselves' in multi-layer configurations. BERT overcomes these limitations through the use of a 'Masked Language Model' (MLM) objective. During pre-training, a random subset of input tokens is masked, and the model is tasked with predicting the original vocabulary ID of the masked word based solely on its surrounding context. This approach enables the representation to integrate both left and right contexts, facilitating the pre-training of a deep bidirectional Transformer.

The Masked Language Model (MLM)

To train a deep bidirectional representation, 15% of the input tokens are masked at random. In contrast to standard directional models, the MLM objective enables the model to attend to the entire sequence, improving its ability to capture complex linguistic relationships. To prevent the model from becoming over-reliant on the '[MASK]' token during fine-tuning—where such masks are absent—the authors employed a specific training strategy: 80% of the target tokens are replaced with the [MASK] token, 10% with a random token, and 10% remain unchanged. This strategy compels the model to maintain a robust contextual representation for every input token.

Next Sentence Prediction (NSP)

Many downstream tasks, such as Question Answering (QA) and Natural Language Inference (NLI), depend on understanding the relationships between sentences, which is not directly captured by standard language modeling. To address this, BERT is pre-trained on a binarized 'next sentence prediction' (NSP) task derived from a monolingual corpus. For each pre-training example, sentence pairs $$A$$ and $$B$$ are selected: 50% of the time, $$B$$ is the actual subsequent sentence for $$A$$, and 50% of the time, it is a random sentence from the corpus. The model is then trained to predict whether the sentences are consecutive.

Fine-Tuning BERT

Fine-tuning BERT is computationally efficient, as all parameters are adjusted jointly by adding a single task-specific output layer. For sequence-level classification, BERT utilizes the representation of the first token in the sequence, known as the special [CLS] token. This unified architecture allows BERT to achieve state-of-the-art results across various benchmarks with minimal task-specific modifications. The model's efficacy was demonstrated by its performance on the GLUE benchmark, the SQuAD question answering dataset, and several other competitive NLP leaderboards.

Concept Breakdown

Bidirectionality

The ability of the model to look at both the preceding and following words in a sequence simultaneously to understand context.

Masked Language Model (MLM)

A pre-training task where certain words in a sentence are hidden, forcing the model to use surrounding context to predict them.

Transformer Encoder

The specific part of the Transformer architecture used by BERT, which focuses on extracting deep contextual features from an input sequence.

Self-Attention

The mechanism that allows BERT to weight the importance of different words in a sentence relative to each other.

[CLS] Token

A special classification token added to the beginning of every sequence, used as the aggregate representation for sequence-level tasks.

Mathematics

Attention Weighting

$$$$\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V$$$$
The core attention mechanism used in each of BERT's layers to compute contextual relationships between tokens.
